Logfile from Beleriand-Malidhrin.

 

 

A feast has begun, one of sight and sound if not yet of taste. For in the great cavern sundered by the rushing Esgalduin, a ring of ellith in splendid dress have gathered about the Queen of Doriath. In the midst of the circle is a large wooden table, on its center a large smooth stone. On either side are fine-weave baskets full of silky grain flour, pots of honey and pitchers of cordial, the makings of the elven waybread whose shaping is reserved for the Bereth and her ladies alone. Yet the implements before them are not its most precious ingredient, rather the purity of spirit with which they endow this ceremony both merry and solemn. A hauntingly beautiful sound rises above the roar of the river far below and the sparkle of the fountain, a song offered by the handmaidens to their mistress, and to Yavanna whose fruits they celebrate, invoking the blessing of both of them over harvest past and long journeys ahead.

 

Malidhrin holds the hands of those maidens standing next to her, joining her voice with the others though more quietly than some. It has not been many seasons that she has partaken of such a ceremony, and she shows her excitement even as the splendor of the occasion brings serenity to her eyes. These she has fastened upon the figure of the Bereth, of adoration and of a keen desire to follow her promptings when it is time to do so.

 

The Bereth's voice is clear, with unearthly loveliness as she sings with her handmaidens in the way she taught them. Her eyes go to each in turn, with maternal affection... her heart and mind perhaps removed to a time when she served at the feet of Yavanna, looking at her mistress the way her maidens are looking at her. The song is of the making of Arda, and Yavanna's part in bringing forth fruits of the earth, of forest and field, a song of harvest and creation and joy.

 

            And holding one Malidhrin's hands is Tinaeriel, and she, too, joins her melody to the song, her own eager anticipation for the work to come manifest in the lilt of her voice as she sings alongside the other maidens. Eyes of blue lie upon the Bereth, shifting to the maiden Malidhrin beside her for but a brief moment afore they return to the Queen, wherein lie the same sort of adoration and desire that might be found in the eyes of her fellow handmaid beside her.

 

 

Malidhrin's smile brightens as the light of Melian's eyes, redolent of the Trees that are no more, rests with fondness upon her. As Melian turns her gaze further onward, the young elleth's eyes turn up to her elder Tinaeriel, gifting her with a shy if warm smile as well. She then glances back as several handmaidens descend from the royal chambers, in their hands the silver-leaf wrappings that will be used to cover the finished lembas. They add their voices to the song as they break the circle briefly, resting the argent coverings on the table with the other supplies.

 

            A smile curves upon, at first just a little, and then spreads across the face of Tinaeriel as the Bereth's eyes are upon her. But as the Queen's deep eyes move farther about the maidens gathered, Tinaeriel again looks to Malidhrin, then smiling upon her perhaps much as an elder sister might to one younger. The song swells slightly with the addition of more maidens as they come, bearing the silvern leaves, and Tinaeriel's voice still joins in the song of utmost beauty.

 

Still singing softly, Melian steps back, placing her hand on one of the bowls. Reverently, but without undue pomp, she holds up the ingredients, each in turn, a and offers them, for the nourishment and well-being of the people of Doriath and the preservation of all that is good and beautiful in the realm. When she has finished, the Bereth ceases singing, and looks around the circle, singling out certain faces -- Malidhrin and Tinaeriel among them -- who have been instructed aforetime to help put together the ingredients of the queen's waybread.

 

At the Bereth's prompting, Malidhrin steps forward, tugging lightly at her companions if only to garner their moral support. Nervously she picks up a crystal jar of honey, as does another handmaiden beside her, the contents of the jars sparkling golden and almost translucent with pure sweetness. She glances to Melian and then back to others of the Silivrieli, awaiting them to spread flour on the stone.

 

            The eyes of the Bereth upon her as a clear indication of her wishes, Tinaeriel steps forward, her footfalls light as ever is her wont. Reaching near where Malidhrin readies the honey, Tinaeriel herself takes up a basket of flour. Her eyes skim across to Malidhrin, then to the Bereth, then down to the stone. There she begins to pour the light and fine powder, a little of it rising from the stone as faint plumes of flour-dust.

 

Others of the handmaidens come forward with their ingredients, waiting for the fine cloud of flour to settle, and begin to pour -- carefully, as they are supposed to know the measurements by sight. One or two of them look up at the Bereth for approval, and she smiles back to them as they finish their part of the task.

 

Malidhrin lifts the pitcher of honey as her companion does the same, and a light stream of the golden liquid caresses the bed of flour. Only a small amount, and then others step forward to pour cordial. In turn they rest the pitchers back on the table. Another song begins as they await the Bereth to put her hand to turning the flour over into the liquids, allowing her a ceremonial first opportunity to touch what will be the life-nourishing wafers.

 

 

            Tinaeriel steps back a bit after setting down the emptied flour-basket with little flourish, her eyes shifting between the Bereth and the ingredients that will soon be turned and mixed together. Joining the new song that begins then is her lightly lilting voice, though now it is softer than before, and the elf-maid's eyes then rest only upon the Bereth.

 

Melian steps forward, stretching out her pale, delicate, strong and perfect hands, gathering the ingredients together before her, the honey and cordial sweetening her hands, or the other way around... She turns the mixture over on the flour, and mixes three times, then steps back, allowing her maidens to help her -- there is a great deal of batter to be made into the winter's store of lembas.

 

Malidhrin puts her hand to the turning of the dough as the Bereth steps back, laughing lightly at both the feel of the dough on her hands and the sight of her fellow handmaidens stepping forward to do the same. Puffs of flour burst out onto lovely gowns, prompting more laughter, though the ellith's movements on the stone are graceful and coordinated. Working together, the simple elements begin to come together into a golden dough that more resembles the wafers known and depended upon by Doriath's wardens, guard, hunters and explorers.

 

            Tinaeriel, too, joins in the mixing, but there is no laughter from her lips as comes from Malidhrin. But there are smiles, aye, bright smiles as the handmaidens together turn the ingredients to dough by the work of their hands. Over and over Tinaeriel mixes the portion of the dough before her, being careful to be sure that all of the elements therein are properly blended.

 

The Bereth herself chuckles quietly -- sacredness does not always mean solemnity, and there is much joy that goes into this task. Melian looks up at Malidhrin, and a laugh escapes her rosebud lips. She giggles -- yes, giggles -- at something on Malidhrin's face, but continues to finish the mixing, which is nearly complete, and the batter smells sweet and inviting already.

 

The Queen's girlish laughter prompts more of the same in her maids, though as their work carries on, chatter and laughter hushes as several maidens enter the hall with trays of a porous stone. Among them are the princesses of the realm, Galadriel and Luthien, the golden fair and she of the twilight, leading this small procession. They exchange smiles with Melian and then with the others. The honor of shaping the first lembas is granted the Queen's daughter, and with a small word of blessing Luthien takes a mass of the dough in her hands, spreading it out on the trays, its rich elastic character allowing it to be spread quite thin. The other maidens look on, and in turn step forward to fill more trays in the same manner, while others take the prepared ones away to the kitchens to be baked. The thin wafers will not require long in baking.

 

            And following in the little procession of maidens who take the dough-laden trays to the kitchens for the baking of the sweet wafers, there is Tinaeriel, who bears one such tray. Smiles still abound throughout the work, for it is pleasurable, and Tinaeriel disappears from the hall as she trails behind the others, carefully balancing the tray in her hands.

 

As the last of the dough is being taken away, other trays of finished lembas are being brought back to the hall by the ellith, their hands wrapped against the heat. When the wafers cool, they will be broken along the lines that were drawn through them before baking. With this new stage of the waybread's making, other Doriathrim have begun to enter the hall, to receive a small piece of the newly made lembas from the ladies' hands. In this way all can share in the joy of the ceremony, even if by tradition not all could share in its work.

 

Malidhrin has remained behind, her arms being yet too delicate to carry the stone trays of bread. She seems fit to break out into a dance, her steps light, and she turns pleased eyes upon the entry of the finished lembas into the hall. She lends her hand to carefully breaking the wafers into squares that will fit the silver leaves in which they are already being wrapped by other maidens' hands.

 

One such person is the towering Warden Hethelkalan. After many, many long hours at the millstone, he feels that he should get a taste of what was part of his labor. But, patiently he waits, softly playing a tune on his panpipes, waiting for a better time, and so that he would not bother the Queen any more that he needs to.

 

Malidhrin carries a silver tray with small pieces of lembas about the hall, offering it to those who have gathered to sample the harvests' goodness. She greets each with a bright smile, the warden Hethelkalan among the others.

 

The queen and her daughter and Galadriel watch the last of these proceedings from one side, as the handmaidens expertly perform the tasks in which they have been instructed. As the door is opened to let others in the city enter to taste the fruits of the royal ladies' labor, the queen graciously murmurs words of welcome and blessing, before letting them sample what has been set aside. She stays to watch and supervise and participate until the last pieces have been wrapped, and put into baskets to be carried away to store or to distribute to wardens at their cold posts above the underground city.
