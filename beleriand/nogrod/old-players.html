ÛMAHAL (DRÓR)
Faction: Narag
Rank: Noble, 1st Class (House Narag)
Liege: King Naugladur Zirak
Profession: Wine Merchant (and Lord Chancellor)
Guild Rank: Master
Description: Ûmahal is an old Dwarf, short and shifty but with an air of some assumed nobility and, certainly, some pride. A grey beard forks from his chin onto his chest, but no lower, for this is no Longbeard, but a Firebeard of Nogrod. He has sharp black eyes and bushy grey eyebrows set in his weatherbeaten face. His hands, too, are calloused and worn.

Ûmahal wears a black tunic, decorated with a great silver seal: the coat of arms of the King of the Hollowbold, which this vassal displays proudly. But over all of this he wears an old grey cloak which covers his arms and back but parts in the front like an overcoat, and the grey hood attached is thrown over his head. You can see baggy woolen black pants upon his legs, and black boots upon his feet.

History: Ûmahal was born in Nogrod, the son of Lord Narag. At this time House Narag had fallen low. Time was when the lord of Narag would be vassal to the King, and Lord Chancellor. No longer. Now Ûmahal's father was vassal to a vassal of Lord Azan, who had risen to fill the positions long ago held by House Narag. Practically the only asset left to House Narag was now the Dolmed's Horn Inn, which, while undoubtedly the best Inn in the Hollowbold, by itself was nothing when compared to the power and riches controlled by Lords Azan and Baraz. As a young (by Dwarven standards) lad, Ûmahal inherited his father's innkeeping business. He was never satisfied with his lot, being not rich enough he said, and too low a vassal in the society of Nogrod. So he set out into the world on merchantile missions, leaving his business in the hands of his serfs and trying to sell all sorts of cheap goods in the faraway markets of Nargothrond, Himring, and especially Doriath. Among other folk he always went by the name of Drór. For many years he spent much of his life divided between the Hollowbold and the Thousand Caverns. As he aged and grew more crafty, he started getting into the wine trade, a profitable one that, selling the fine wine of the faraway southern land of Dor-Winion to the eager markets of Elves in the north. Though at first he had been a bit of a swindler, so what? Now he was an established, respectable merchant, climbing his way up through the ranks of Nogrod's capitalistic feudalism, and now he is himself a vassal of Dinazan, Lord Azan himself. Now Dinazan and he were cousins of some sort. Seems somewhere along the line there Dinazan's ancestor had been the upright, successful older brother, and Ûmahal's ancestor had been the black sheep of the family. And though Dinazan respected his cousin's success, he still perhaps felt towards him as an older brother feels for a younger: a little scornfully. Ûmahal had never been the upright aristocrat and statesman that Dinazan had been.Still, what with one thing or the next, Ûmahal somehow managed to climb near the top. And with Dinazan's disappearance and assumed death or capture in battle with Angband near Himring, Ûmahal was the most obvious choice for leading the Black faction and stepping in as Lord Chancellor to the King of Nogrod. Less pompus than old Dinazan he is, though less capable some argue. But the Dwarves call him Lord, and that's how things be until Dinazan returns. And does that look like it'll happen? Hasn't yet. Been maybe five years now. So Ûmahal does as he likes, and he's getting on in years, a respectable old Dwarf he is.
